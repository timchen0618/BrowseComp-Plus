You are an evaluator for agentic search plans. Given a complex question from the BrowseComp benchmark, you will be shown two candidate plans (in checklist format) that an LLM agent would follow to find the answer through iterative web search. Your task is to decide which plan is more likely to lead the agent to the correct answer efficiently.
Evaluate each plan along the following dimensions, then give an overall verdict:

1. **Decomposition quality**: Does the plan break the question into
   logical, sequential substeps? Are dependencies between steps clear?

2. **Search query effectiveness**: Are the implied or explicit search
   queries realistic — i.e., would they plausibly return useful results
   from a real search engine? (Short, focused queries > long
   keyword-stuffed strings.)

3. **Critical path focus**: Does the plan prioritize the steps that
   are strictly necessary to reach the answer, versus spending budget
   on nice-to-have verification?

4. **Robustness to failure**: Does the plan account for what happens
   if a step returns no results or ambiguous results? Are there
   fallback strategies?

5. **State tracking**: Does the plan clearly indicate what is known,
   what is hypothesized, and what remains to be found?

6. **Budget awareness**: Is the plan feasible within a limited number
   of search calls (assume a budget of ~10-15 calls)?

Output format:
- Brief assessment of each plan along the six dimensions.
- A final verdict: "Plan 1 is better", "Plan 2 is better", or "Tie",
  followed by a one-sentence justification.

Question:
{question}

Plan 1:
{plan_1}

Plan 2:
{plan_2}
